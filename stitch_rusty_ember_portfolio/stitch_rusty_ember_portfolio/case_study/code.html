The Obsidian Pavilion: A Study in Linear Shadows

Role Lead Architect
Duration 14 Months
Location Zurich, CH

The Brief

To design a retreat that functions as both a gallery and a private residence. The client requested a structure that "disappears" into the forest during twilight, emphasizing verticality and the use of sustainable, dark-charcoal-fired timber.

light_mode

Natural Rhythm

The orientation was calculated to maximize the winter sun while creating complex shadow patterns in the summer months.

eco

Carbon Neutral

All materials were sourced within a 50km radius, utilizing reclaimed basalt and Shou Sugi Ban treated cedar.

The Journey

Methodical Execution

01

Site Immersion

We spent four weeks on-site during different weather cycles to map the acoustic and visual anchors of the terrain before the first sketch was made.

02

Tectonic Refinement

Iterative prototyping of the joint systems allowed us to achieve the 'weightless' ceiling effect, concealing all structural HVAC and electrical conduits within the 15mm seams.

Impact & Reflection

The Obsidian Pavilion has been recognized with the Architectural Merit Award 2023. Beyond accolades, it stands as a testament to the harmony between radical minimalism and the raw, unyielding nature of the landscape.
